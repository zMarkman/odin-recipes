Zach's Simple Miso Ramen

Delicious veg ramen made easy.

Have you ever wanted to eat a super delicious ramen? You're in luck!

This ramen is GOATed.

I came up with this during my vegan days as a way to minimize my processed food intake.

Ask any vegan or vegetarian and they might tell you how hard it is to get enough vitamins, minerals, and proteins by only eating plants and plant-based products.

I didn't want to set my money on fire at Whole Foods buying prepared broths or just-add-water cups, so I set out to Google my way towards a tasty (and somewhat healthy) homemade dinner.

The first element of a good ramen is a good dashi, but that has bonito fish flakes in it!

So, I started by searching for a vegetarian dashi and found out that you can make one with kombu and/or wakame seaweeds instead! It's not the same as fish flakes, but if you use shiitake powder or boil in small pieces of sliced shiitake mushrooms you can create a savory base that is animal-free.

Once the dashi boils, set the water to a simmer and gently sift your miso in. I like to use 1tbsp miso per 1cup of water in the dashi with just a few sprinkles of wakame and one big piece of kombu which I remove before serving. Kombu is not a part of this dish beyond boiling it for dashi. I also add 0.5 tbsp of sesame oil per 1cup water, as well as 1tbsp soy sauce (ideally tamari) per 1cup water.

Once the broth is simmering, I add in tofu, mushrooms, veggies, and anything else that sounds good in a salty soup! Mix together in a ramen bowl with noodles that you boiled separately ahead of time and enjoy!

Ingredients

  • water
  • 1 piece kombu
  • sprinkle of wakame
  • yellow miso
  • soy sauce
  • shiitake mushrooms
  • tofu
  • sesame oil
  • veggies
  • ramen noodles

    • Steps

    1. Fill pot with water
    2. Add wakame and/or kombu
    3. Bring water to boil
    4. Reduce to simmer, add miso
    5. Add soy sauce, sesame oil
    6. Add veggies, mushrooms, tofu
    7. Mix in bowl with cooked noodles
    8. Enjoy!
