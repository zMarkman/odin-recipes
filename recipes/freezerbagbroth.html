Simple Veggie Scrap Broth

Save scraps. Free broth forever.

Money is hard to come by.

Between rent, food, and having fun on a Friday night with friends, money goes out of your hands quick.

Come with me on a journey towards financial freedom (or a step on that journey) by way of the magic broth road!

The secret is waste.

Food waste, that is... which isn't necessarily waste at all!

Unlike "recycling" which often simply gets set on fire, much of your food waste can actually be reused.

Here's what I do to save money.

Any time I make a meal using vegetables, which is, you know a decent but not universal amount of time, I save any scraps of root vegetables, mushrooms, garlic, etc. and put them in a ziploc bag in the freezer.

After awhile the bag gets full. At that point, I dump all the scraps into an Instant Pot, fill it with water to the max fill line, then set it to manual for 30 minutes.

This is typically enough time for all the flavorful bits of my frozen scraps to be pressure-cooked into the water.

Voila! Pour the broth through a strainer to remove the scraps, which you can now dispose of.

Re-freeze the broth for use whenever you need something more flavorful than water in a recipe.

Ingredients

  • Water
  • Vegetable/mushroom scraps

Steps

  1. Save scraps in a freezer bag as you make vegetable dishes
  2. Freeze scraps in a ziploc bag
  3. When bag full, put scraps in Instant Pot
  4. Fill Instant Pot with water to max fill line
  5. Turn Instant Pot on to manual for 30 minutes
  6. Drain broth into pyrex through strainer
  7. Freeze or use broth and discard the scraps
